    • Abroad
      • Found: Fresh Clues to Mystery of King Solomon's Mines
        • From National Geographic
        • three thousand year old manure in the Timna Valley of Israel was discovered during the excavation of an ancient mining camp.
        • There are remnants of several wall structures there.
        • This was previously considered to be a Bronze Age site - tied into the New Kingdom of Egypt in the 13th Century BC.
        • However, carbon-dating appears to show that it was active in the 10th century BC - the time of David and Solomon.
        • The industrial level of copper production at the site, along with the seeds & pollen found in the dung, show that this was very possibly part of a large-scale, long-distance trade arrangement.

    • Knights of Columbus - Gabe
      • Intro
        • Motto - "In service to One, In service to all."
        • Age - 135 years old
        • Membership Requirements
          • Catholic
          • Men
          • 18 years old
      • History
        • Founding
          • Catholic priest, Father Michael McGivney
          • 1882
          • Connecticut
          • Originally inspired by the need to take care of widows and orphans in immigrant communities
          • support structure for Catholics (ostracized by American society at the time)
          • alternative to Masonry due to Papal prohibition for Catholics to be Masons
          • Namesake is Christopher Columbus, both as hope that one day, Catholics wouldn't be treated the way they were being treated, and as a reminder to both Catholics and Protestants that Catholics were a key part in the birth of the US.
        • Early 20th Century Oath Hoax
          • Anti-Catholic prejudice
          • Old oath previously falsely attributed to the Jesuits
          • Circulated primarily by the Klu Klux Klan in an anti-Catholic effort
          • Summary
            • denouces authority of Protestant government leaders
            • generally anti-Protestant
            • anti-Mason
            • spying on Protestants using Catholic women
            • allegiance only to the Pope
            • "I do further promise and declare that I will, when opportunity presents, make and wage relentless war, secretly and openly against all heretics, Protestants and Masons, as I am directed to do to extirpate them from the face of the whole earth; and that I will spare neither age, sex, or condition, and that will hang, bum, waste, boil, flay, strangle, and bury alive these infamous heretics; rip up the stomachs and wombs of their women, and crush their infants' heads against the wails in order to annihilate their execrable race. That when the same can not be done openly, I will secretly use the poisonous cup, the strangulation cord, the steel of the poniard, or the leaden bullet, regardless of the honor, rank, dignity, or authority of the persons, whatever may be their condition in life, either public or private, as I at any time may be directed so to do by any agents of the Pope or superior of the Brotherhood of the Holy Father of the Society of Jesus."
          • Again, fake oath, circulated by the Klan, stop quoting it.
        • Membership
          • 2 years after starting the Order - 459 members
          • 7 years - 40,000 members
          • 20 years - 230,000 members
          • Present day - more than 1.9 million members
          • membership has grown every year for about 45 years straight
      • Values
        • Charity
        • Unity
        • Fraternity
        • Patriotism
      • Structure
        • Individual Level
          • Organized into councils
          • Presided over by a "Worthy" Grand Knight
          • Degrees
            • First Order / First Degree Knight - Charity
            • Second Order / Second Degree Knight - Unity
            • Third Order / Full Membership - Fraternity
            • Fourth Order
              • Optional
              • Patriotism
              • About a fifth of KoC are also members of the Fourth Order
              • Drill teams, parades, color guards
              • kind of like commandery
              • organized into assemblies
              • presided over by a "Faithful" Navigator
        • State/National/International Level
          • Governed by the Supreme Council, which is made up of members from every KoC jurisdiction
      • Associated Organizations
        • "Parent" Organizatons
          • member of the International Alliance of Catholic Knights (an international organization of 15 Roman Catholic fraternities).
        • Women's Auxilaries
          • Daughters of Isabella
          • Catholic Daughters of the Americas
        • Youth Groups
          • Columbian Squires (boys)
          • Squire Roses (girls)

          • they give over $175 million a year and about 74 million volunteer hours
          • Habitat for Humanity
          • Coat donation programs
          • Blood drives
          • Scholarship programs for people going into religious vocations
          • Pacem in Terris Fund - Catholic Church efforts towards peace in Middle East
          • Special Olympics
          • Global Wheelchair fund
          • Disaster relief (earthquakes, hurricanes, etc)
        • Insurance
          • life insurance

          • most highly rated program in America
        • Evangelization
          • Catholic Information Service

        • Politics

    • Odd Fellows - Gabe
      • Intro
        • Specific Odd Fellows discussed is the Independant Order of Odd Fellows (USA)
          • UK - Grand United Order of Oddfellows
          • UK - splinter organization Independent Order of Oddfellows Manchester Unity (the parent organization of IOOF)
          • American people of color - Grand United Order of Odd Fellows in America
          • But for simplicity, we're talking about the IOOF / Independant Order of Odd Fellows
        • Motto - Friendship, Love and Truth
        • Age - 197 years
        • Membership Requirements
          • Men and women
          • Must believe in a Supreme Being - specifically a Creator
          • Usually must be 16 or older
      • History
        • Founding
          • Unofficially existed in NYC in the early 1800s
          • Washington Lodge #1 was charted in 1819 and affiliated with the Independant Order of Oddfellows Manchester Unity in 1820
        • Schisms
          • The American Lodges split in 1842 due to the ritual being changed by the British Lodges without the agreement of the American lodges
        • Membership
          • Began accepting womeen in 1851
          • Nearly destroyed in the Civil War (membership)
          • Came back strong and from 1860 to the 1910s, they were the largest fraternal organization in the US.
          • Peak membership of 2.7 million in 1921
          • Steadily declining since then
          • removed the whites-only clause in 1971
          • 1979 - 243,000 members
          • bouncing back in the 21st century - currently about 600,000 members
      • Values
        • Friendship
        • Love
        • Truth
        • Faith
        • Hope
        • Charity
        • Universal Justice
      • Structure
        • the Lodge
          • Based on Friendship, Love, and Truth
          • 1st Degree - Friendship
          • 2nd Degree - Fraternal Love
          • 3rd Degree - Truth
          • Presided over by the Noble Grand
        • the Encampment
          • Based on Faith, Hope, and Charity
          • Appendent body open to 3rd degree members in good standing with their Lodge
          • 1st Degree - Patriarch
          • 2nd Degree - Golden Rule
          • 3rd Degree - Royal Purple
          • Presided over by the Chief Patriarch or Chief Matriarch
        • the Patriarchs Militant
          • Founded during Civil War
          • Chivalric (like Commandery)
          • Organized into Cantons
          • One degree - Chevalier
          • Must be in good standing with Lodge and Encampment
          • Presided over by a Commandant
        • State/National/International Level
          • Regional Grand Lodges
          • Jurisdictional Grand Lodges (Countries/States)
      • Associated Organizations
        • Women's Auxilaries
          • The Daughters of Rebekah
          • Ladies of the Orient
        • Youth Groups
          • Daughters of Rebekah
          • Junior Lodges
        • Honorary/Optional Groups
          • Ancient Mystic Order of Samaritans (basically IOOF Shrine based on the story of Xerxes)
      • Activities
        • Scholarships and low-interest student loans
        • Annual pilgramage to the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier
        • Spend over $775 million in relief projects every year
        • SOS Children's Village - orphan homes internationally
        • Living Legacy - tree-planting
        • Arthiritis Foundation
        • Supporting vision care and research
        • Odd Fellow & Rebekah homes for the elderly and orphans
      • Famous Members
        • Charlie Chaplin
        • Wyatt Earp
        • Anson Jones, Last President of the Republic of Texas
        • Charles Lindbergh
        • Sir John A. Macdonald, 1st Prime Minister of Canada (also a Mason)
