    • Knights of Columbus (GABE)
      • Who?
        • Organization Name
          • Named after Christopher Columbus
          • Knights of Columbus councils, Fourth Degree assemblies,, and Columbian Squire circles.
        • Famous Members
          • President John F. Kennedy
          • Samuel Alito
          • Jeb Bush
          • Vince Lombardi
          • Babe Ruth
        • Closely Tied Organizations
          • The Catholic Church
          • The International Alliance of Catholic Knights - an organization of fifteen Roman Catholic fraternal societies from 27 different countries.
          • Daughters of Isabella
          • The Columbian Squires - male youth organization, akin to DeMolay
          • The Squire Roses - same deal, but for girls.
      • Why?
        • Stated purpose and Values
          • Their values are Charity, Unity, Fraternity and Patriotism
          • Each value is exemplified by one of the four degrees.
        • Origin / response
          • McGivney wanted to be able to provide insurance to the widows and children of American Roman Catholics and Roman Catholic immigrants
          • They chose Columbus as a namesake in hope that Catholics would someday be accepted as regular Americans, and as a reminder that Catholics were instrumental in the establishment of America
      • What?
        • What do they do?
          • Charity
            • Over $175 million to charity a year (and rising)
            • 74 million man hours (and rising)
            • Habitat for Humanity
            • Coat donation programs
            • Blood drives
            • Scholarship programs for people going into religious vocations
            • Pacem in Terris Fund - Catholic Church efforts towards peace in Middle East
            • Special Olympics
            • Global Wheelchair fund
            • Disaster relief (earthquakes, hurricanes, etc)
          • Insurance Program
            • life insurance
            • long term care insurance
            • disability insurance
            • most highly rated program in America
          • Evangelization
            • booklets
            • correspondance courses
          • Politics
            • Not allowed to engage in partisan activity, only issue-related activity
            • Lobbied to add "under God" to the Pledge of Allegiance in 1954.
            • Campaign against gay marriage
            • Campaign against abortion
        • What are their rituals?
          • The First Degree exemplifies the virtue of Charity.
          • The Second Degree exemplifies the virtue of Unity.
          • The Third Degree teaches Fraternity and makes the recipient a full member and Knight.
          • There is an optional Fourth Degree focusing on Patriotism.
            • Members of the Fourth Degree are still members of a Knights of Columbus council, but they are also members of a Fourth Degree Assembly.
            • Less than one-fifth of Knights become Fourth Degree Members.
            • Assemblies have Color Corps groups that march in parades.
            • Their emblem is a dove and globe superimposed upon a cross.
          • Fake obligation:
            • "I do promise and declare that I will, when opportunity presents, make and wage relentless war, secretly and openly, against all heretics, Protestants and Masons, as I am directed to do, to extirpate them from the face of the whole earth; and that I will spare neither age, sex, nor condition, and that I will hang, burn, waste, boil, flay, strangle, and bury alive those infamous heretics; rip up the stomachs and wombs of their women, and crush their infants' heads against the walls in order to annihilate their execrable race...."
            • Anti-Catholic Proganda from the early 1900s.
            • Still used to rile Masons up against KoC or just aggravate people
            • The Catholic church still considers being a Mason a grave sin, though.
      • When?
        • When did they organize/start?
          • Founded in 1881 by Father Michael McGivney in New Haven, Connecticut
        • What's membership look like today?
          • Still growing
          • Almost 2 million members/li>
          • Membership has grown each year for 44 years
      • Where?
        • Where are they based?
          • US, Puerto Rico, Virgin Islands, Guam, Canada, the Philippines, Mexico, Poland, the Dominican Republic, Panama, the Bahamas, Cuba, Guatemala, Saipan, South Korea, Ukraine, Lithuania, and countless US military bases.
        • Where do you go to learn more?
          • www.KofC.org
