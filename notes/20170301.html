    • There is now an effort to raise funds for the restoration of the building called the Colored Masonic Temple in Birmingham, Alabama. The building was opened in 1924 by the Most Worshipful Prince Hall Grand Lodge, Free & Accepted Masons of Alabama. It’s been the home of many organizations – obviously the MWPHGLFAMoAL, but also offices for doctors, lawyers, accountants, a barbershop, billiards room, and most notably the NAACP. Duke Ellington and Count Basie regularly played there. In the segregated South, the building was seen as a symbol of black prosperity, and today is considered to be a major landmark in the Civil Rights Movement. The MWPHGLFAMoAL is working in conjuction with the National Park Service to try and get the building restored. There is a GoFundMe for the effort to remove the lead paint and asbestos from the building. The Grand Lodge is hoping to get federal and state historic tax credits for the renovation. Best of luck to the Grand Lodge and MWGM Hawkins.
